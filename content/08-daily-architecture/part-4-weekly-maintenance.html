Weekly Maintenance

System updates and backups that prevent monthly crises

The Compound Interest of Maintenance

Daily routines keep you going. Weekly maintenance keeps you growing. Without regular maintenance, small issues compound into major failures. The email inbox becomes unmanageable. The project becomes misaligned. Health habits slip. Relationships drift. Weekly maintenance is the difference between reactive crisis management and proactive excellence.

Weekly Maintenance is scheduled system optimization.
It's the weekly review that prevents monthly chaos.
It's the relationship check-in that prevents disconnection.
It's the financial review that prevents financial stress.
It's the health assessment that prevents health crises.
Small weekly investments prevent massive future costs.

Most people wait for things to break before fixing them. This is expensive, stressful, and inefficient. Weekly maintenance fixes things while they're still small, cheap, and easy to handle.

The Four Maintenance Categories

Operational Maintenance

Keeping the machine running. Email inbox zero, task system review, calendar planning, workspace organization. This is the "change the oil" of your productivity system.

Strategic Maintenance

Keeping aligned with direction. Progress review against goals, learning integration, course correction, big picture thinking. This is the "check the map" of your journey.

Personal Maintenance

Keeping the operator healthy. Health metrics, exercise planning, meal prep, sleep review, stress management. This is the "maintain the driver" of your life vehicle.

Relational Maintenance

Keeping connections strong. Relationship check-ins, social planning, gratitude expressions, conflict resolution. This is the "lubricate the joints" of your social system.

The Weekly Review Protocol (90 minutes)

Same time every week. Protected time. Non-negotiable.

1

Capture & Collect (15 minutes)

Gather all loose ends. Empty physical inbox, collect notes from everywhere, download brain downloads, capture random thoughts. Get everything into your system.

2

Process & Organize (30 minutes)

Clarify and categorize. Process all captured items, clarify next actions, file reference material, update project lists. Transform chaos into order.

3

Review & Reflect (30 minutes)

Look backward and forward. Review completed week, celebrate wins, identify improvements. Review upcoming week, set priorities, anticipate challenges.

4

Plan & Prepare (15 minutes)

Set up for success. Schedule big rocks for next week, prepare environment, communicate needs, create shopping lists. Make next week easy before it starts.

The Friday Afternoon Advantage

Friday afternoon (3-5pm) is the ideal weekly review time. The week's work is fresh in mind, but the weekend hasn't started. Completing the review creates psychological closure for the week and sets up Monday for immediate productivity. Monday morning you're executing, not planning.

The Maintenance Dashboard

What gets measured gets maintained. Create a simple one-page dashboard.

Without Dashboard (Flying Blind)

  • Health declines slowly, unnoticed
  • Finances drift without tracking
  • Projects stall without review
  • Relationships fade without attention
  • Result: Surprise crises, slow decline

With Dashboard (Clear Visibility)

  • Health metrics tracked weekly
  • Financial position reviewed weekly
  • Project progress measured weekly
  • Relationship quality assessed weekly
  • Result: Proactive adjustments, continuous improvement

Dashboard Categories (Weekly Check)

Health: Sleep average, exercise frequency, nutrition quality, stress level (1-10)
Wealth: Income, expenses, savings rate, investments
Work: Projects completed, goals progress, skills developed
Relationships: Quality time with key people, conflicts resolved, appreciation expressed
Growth: Books read, lessons learned, new experiences
Contribution: How you helped others, impact created

The Weekly Planning Ritual

How to structure your week for maximum effectiveness and balance.

This Week's Maintenance Implementation

  1. Maintenance Time Block: Schedule 90 minutes this week for your first weekly review. Protect it like a doctor's appointment.
  2. Dashboard Creation: Create a simple one-page dashboard with your 6 key categories.
  3. Review Protocol Practice: Follow the 4-step weekly review protocol exactly once.
  4. Big Rocks Scheduling: Identify 3-5 "big rocks" (important tasks) for next week and schedule them first.
  5. Maintenance Habit Stacking: Attach weekly maintenance to an existing habit (e.g., after Friday lunch, before Sunday dinner).

Weekly maintenance feels like "extra work" until you experience the compound benefits: fewer emergencies, less stress, clearer direction, more progress. It's the highest-leverage time investment you can make.
