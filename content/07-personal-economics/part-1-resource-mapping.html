Resource Mapping

The forensic audit of your personal balance sheet

The First Law of Personal Economics

You cannot manage what you don't measure. Most people are running a personal economy with no balance sheet, no profit/loss statement, and no understanding of their actual resource flows. They wonder why they're bankrupt in time, energy, and attention while chasing money alone.

Resource Mapping is the forensic audit
of your four core currencies:
Time · Energy · Attention · Capital
It's understanding what you actually have,
where it's actually going,
and what's actually leaking.

This isn't about tracking minutes or dollars. It's about understanding the exchange rates between different forms of capital and identifying the memory leaks in your personal operating system.

The Four Resource Classes

Temporal Capital

Your non-renewable currency. 168 hours per week, fixed supply. The ultimate scarcity. Most people treat time as infinite while hoarding money. The wealthy understand this inversion.

Energy Reserves

Your daily processing power. Renewable but limited per cycle. Sleep, nutrition, and stress management determine your available units. Energy bankruptcy is more crippling than financial bankruptcy.

Attention Allocation

Your deployable focus capital. The most valuable resource in the attention economy. Every notification, every distraction, every context switch is a withdrawal from this account.

Financial Capital

Your stored value token. The most measurable but least important in isolation. Useful only when converted into other resources. Money is a means, never an end.

The 7-Day Forensic Audit

For one week, track everything. Not with obsessive detail, but with strategic awareness.

1

Time Logging

Track your 168 hours in 6 categories: Sleep, Work, Maintenance, Learning, Relationships, Creation. Use a simple spreadsheet. No judgment, just observation.

2

Energy Mapping

Rate your energy on a 1-10 scale 3x daily. Note what drains you (energy vampires) and what fills you (energy generators). Identify patterns.

3

Attention Tracking

Every time you switch tasks, note the trigger. Every time you reach for your phone, note the reason. Map your attention fractures throughout the day.

4

Capital Flow Analysis

Track not just what you spend, but what you're trading for money. Your hourly rate isn't just dollars—it's the exchange of time, energy, and attention for currency.

The Revealing Insight

Most people discover they're spending their highest-value resources (attention, peak energy) on lowest-value returns. They trade creative morning energy for social media scrolling. They exchange focused attention for multitasking meetings. They spend prime temporal capital on administrative tasks.

Identifying Your Memory Leaks

In computing, memory leaks are resources allocated but never released. In personal economics, they're activities that consume resources without proportional return.

Common Memory Leaks

  • Decision Fatigue: 100+ micro-decisions daily
  • Context Switching: Task switching costs
  • Low-Value Consumption: Entertainment as default
  • Administrative Creep: Systems maintenance overhead
  • Relationship Drains: Emotional labor without reciprocity

Resource Recovery

  • Decision Batching: Weekly meal planning
  • Deep Work Blocks: Protected focus time
  • High-Value Input: Educational content
  • Automation: Systems that run themselves
  • Boundaried Giving: Reciprocity enforcement

The Resource Exchange Matrix

Every activity is a resource exchange. Watching Netflix isn't "free time"—it's trading 2 hours of temporal capital + attention allocation for entertainment value. The question isn't "Do I have time?" but "Is this the best exchange rate for my resources?"

This Week's Project: Build Your Balance Sheet

Resource Audit Worksheet

  1. Time Allocation: Map your 168 hours. What percentage goes to each category?
  2. Energy Audit: List your top 3 energy drains and top 3 energy generators.
  3. Attention Log: Count your task switches in a 4-hour work block.
  4. Capital Conversion: Calculate your true hourly rate including all resource costs.
  5. Memory Leak Identification: List 3 resource leaks you can plug this week.

Don't optimize yet. Just observe. The map must be accurate before you navigate.
