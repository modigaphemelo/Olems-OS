Shipping Anyway

The deployment protocol

The Courage to Be Wrong Publicly

Better to ship flawed than to perfect nothing. The world is filled with perfect ideas that never saw daylight and mediocre creations that changed everything because they actually shipped.

Perfectionism is the armor of the scared. "It's not ready yet" is the battle cry of people who never win battles. Shipping is the skill that matters most - the ability to take something from your private imagination to public reality, flaws and all.

Your version 1.0 will embarrass your version 2.0. That's not failure - that's progress.

The 70% Solution Principle

If you have 70% of the information and feel 70% confident, you should ship.

Mathematical Advantage

Waiting for 100% certainty means infinite delay. 70% confidence with immediate shipping beats 100% confidence with permanent hesitation.

The Iteration Edge

Someone who ships at 70% and iterates will far outperform someone who waits for perfection that never arrives.

Reality Feedback

Real-world feedback from an imperfect ship is more valuable than theoretical perfection from endless polishing.

Momentum Creation

Shipping creates momentum. Momentum creates opportunities. Opportunities create success. It all starts with that first imperfect release.

The Launch Principle

Start before you're ready

If you wait until you feel ready, you'll never start. The feeling of readiness often comes after you ship, not before.

The Deployment Protocol

1

Define "Shippable"

What's the absolute minimum that provides value? Not "perfect." Not "complete." Shippable. Define this clearly before you start building.

2

Set Hard Deadlines

Not "when it's ready." A specific date. Time constraints force completion. Parkinson's Law: work expands to fill time available.

3

Build to the Deadline

When the deadline hits, ship whatever you have. No extensions. No "just one more feature." Ship.

4

Gather Real Feedback

What actually happens when real people use it? This data is worth 1000x your speculation.

5

Iterate Based on Data

Now you know what actually matters. Improve based on reality, not theory.

The Sovereign Economy

Sovereignty not spent is sovereignty compounded. Every ship - every public creation - adds to your sovereignty capital.

Attention Capital

Where your focus goes grows. Shipping directs attention to creation, not consumption.

Decision Capital

Each shipping decision strengthens your decision-making muscle. Each hesitation weakens it.

Position Capital

Each ship establishes territory in the world. Territory compounds into influence.

Influence Capital

Sovereignty demonstrated attracts similar minds. Influence compounds as you create domains others wish to join.

The Compound Principle

Each ship makes the next ship easier. Each public creation builds your evidence portfolio. Each iteration compounds your competence. Shipping isn't a single event - it's a compounding machine for personal sovereignty.

Your Shipping Protocol

Define Your First Ship

What's one thing you could ship this week? Define "shippable" for it. Set a hard deadline.

Apply the 70% Solution

Is it 70% ready? Ship it. Not 100%. Not 90%. 70%.

Build Your Shipping Muscle

Make shipping a habit. Small ships weekly. Medium ships monthly. Large ships quarterly.
