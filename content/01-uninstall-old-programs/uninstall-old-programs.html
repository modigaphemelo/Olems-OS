01. Uninstall Old Programs

The first law: Stop running what doesn't serve me

Before installing new software,
you must remove the malware.
Before writing new code,
you must delete the corrupted files.
This is the digital equivalent of a factory reset.

The first law of my new operating system:
"Stop running what doesn't serve me."

The Core Principle

Your life is currently running dozens of background processes you didn't consciously install. Habits inherited from family. Thought patterns absorbed from culture. Behaviors copied from peers. These are the malware of existence - programs that consume your processing power, memory, and battery life without your permission.

Uninstall Old Programs is the forensic audit of your current system. It's identifying every running process and asking: "Does this serve my purpose?" If the answer is no, it gets uninstalled. Not disabled. Not minimized. Deleted.
