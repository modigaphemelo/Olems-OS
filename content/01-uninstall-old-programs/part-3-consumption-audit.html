Consumption Audit

A forensic examination of what you let in

The Input Problem

Your mind is not a vault. It's a processing plant. What goes in determines what comes out. Garbage in, garbage out. Excellence in, excellence out. Yet most people are meticulous about food intake and reckless about mental intake.

The consumption audit asks: "What are you feeding your mind, and why?" Soccer highlights, porn scenes, political arguments, celebrity gossip, binge-watched series - this is mental junk food. It tastes good going down but provides zero nutrition and creates future health problems.

You wouldn't eat McDonald's for every meal. Why consume mental McDonald's?

The Five Categories of Mental Consumption

Entertainment Extraction

Soccer, TV shows, movies, video games. Designed to hijack attention through narrative and emotion. The modern equivalent of Roman bread and circuses - pacification through spectacle.

Audit Question: "Does watching others achieve things help me achieve mine?"

Sexual Simulation

Porn, erotic content, romantic fantasies. Hijacks the reproductive drive for commercial gain. Creates unrealistic expectations, damages real relationships, wastes creative energy.

Audit Question: "Does this simulated intimacy improve my capacity for real connection?"

Chemical Escape

Alcohol, drugs, excessive caffeine. Artificial mood alteration that creates dependency. Temporarily numbs pain while preventing its actual solution. Borrows happiness from tomorrow.

Audit Question: "Am I solving a problem or postponing it?"

Information Obesity

News, social media, podcasts, articles. The illusion of learning without actual comprehension or application. Data without wisdom. Knowing scores without understanding the game.

Audit Question: "Will this information improve my decisions or just fill my head?"

Social Consumption

Gossip, small talk, complaints, drama. Emotional fast food. Provides social calories without nutritional value. Bonding over shared negativity rather than shared growth.

Audit Question: "Is this connection deepening or draining?"

The Seven-Day Forensic Audit

1

Track Everything

For seven days, record every minute of consumption: what, when, duration, and emotional state before/after. Use a notes app or physical journal. No judgment, just observation.

2

Categorize

Sort each consumption event into the five categories. Calculate totals: hours of entertainment, sexual content, chemical intake, information, social consumption.

3

Apply the Benefit Matrix

Score each category against the six currencies (energy, attention, peace, skill, relationships, autonomy). Create a consumption report card.

4

Calculate the Tax

For each hour consumed, calculate the invisible tax: cognitive load, emotional residue, identity alignment, opportunity cost. See the true price.

Typical Audit Results

The Soccer Fan

15 hours weekly: matches (6h), highlights (3h), analysis (3h), discussions (3h). Benefit score: -45. Opportunity cost: one full workday of creative output lost. Identity tax: 20% of self-worth tied to strangers' performance.

The Series Binger

20 hours weekly: Netflix/streaming. Benefit score: -60. Skill equity: zero. Emotional residue: fictional drama affecting real mood. Reality perception: distorted by narrative structures.

The Social Scroller

10 hours weekly: Instagram/Twitter/TikTok. Benefit score: -30. Attention fragmentation: severe. Comparison anxiety: high. Creative output: near zero. Real relationships: neglected.

The Third Insight

The audit reveals a simple truth: most of what you consume doesn't serve you. It serves advertisers, content creators, sports leagues, pharmaceutical companies, social platforms.

You are the product being sold. Your attention is the commodity. Your emotional reactions are the fuel. Your time is the payment.

The audit is the moment you stop being the product and start being the producer.
