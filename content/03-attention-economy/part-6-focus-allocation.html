Focus Allocation

The Economics of Attention Capital

Attention spent is life spent.
Your focus is deployable capital.
This is the strategic allocation of your most precious resource.

In Parts 1-5, we defended against extraction and cultivated depth. Now we master allocation. Focus allocation is the art of deploying your attention capital for maximum return. Not just protecting focus, but investing it wisely. This is portfolio management for your cognitive resources.

The Allocation Principle

Most people treat attention as expense - something that gets spent. Sovereigns treat attention as capital - something that gets invested. The difference between spending and investing changes everything.

The Attention Portfolio Framework

Asset Class 1: Growth Investments

High risk, high return: Learning new skills, exploring new domains, creative projects. These may fail but offer exponential returns if successful.

Example: Learning a new language, starting a business.
Allocation: 20-30% of attention capital

Asset Class 2: Core Holdings

Stable, reliable returns: Maintaining health, deepening existing skills, nurturing relationships. The foundation that everything else builds upon.

Example: Daily exercise, skill maintenance, family time.
Allocation: 40-50% of attention capital

Asset Class 3: Defensive Assets

Preservation, not growth: Administration, maintenance, obligations. Necessary but not growth-oriented. Minimize but don't eliminate.

Example: Taxes, emails, chores, meetings.
Allocation: 20-30% of attention capital

Calculating Return on Attention (ROA)

The ROA Formula

ROA = (Value Created × Time Horizon) ÷ Attention Invested

  • Value Created: What does this attention produce? (Skills, relationships, income, knowledge)
  • Time Horizon: How long do returns last? (Minutes, days, years, lifetime)
  • Attention Invested: How much focus does it require? (Hours, mental energy)

ROA Examples

Activity Value Created Time Horizon Attention ROA
Learning to code High (skill) Years 100 hours Very High
Scrolling social media Low (entertainment) Minutes 1 hour Very Low
Exercising High (health) Years 30 min High
Watching TV Medium (relaxation) Hours 2 hours Low

The ROA Insight

Most people invest attention in low-ROA activities because they're easy. Sovereigns deliberately choose high-ROA activities even when they're hard. The compounding effect is staggering over years.

The Weekly Allocation Ritual

1

Sunday Evening: Portfolio Review

Review past week's attention allocation. Plot activities on matrix. Calculate approximate ROA. Note patterns and leaks.

Exercise: Color-code your calendar by attention type.
2

Monday Morning: Allocation Planning

Plan the week's attention allocation. Schedule Quadrant 1 blocks first. Designate time for each asset class. Set intention for each day.

Exercise: Block 3+ hours daily for Quadrant 1 activities.
3

Daily: Micro-Allocation Checks

Before each activity: "What's the ROA? Which quadrant?" During transitions: "Am I allocating as planned?" Evening: Quick allocation check.

Exercise: Set hourly chimes for allocation awareness.
4

Weekly: Rebalancing

Like an investment portfolio, rebalance when allocations drift. Too much in defensive assets? Too little in growth? Adjust accordingly.

Exercise: Calculate your weekly allocation percentages.

Attention compounds. An hour spent learning compounds into skills that create value for years. An hour spent scrolling disappears forever. The difference seems small daily but becomes astronomical over decades. Your attention allocation today determines your life trajectory.
